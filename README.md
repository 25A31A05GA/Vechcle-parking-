Vehicle parking is the act of stopping and disengaging a vehicle and leaving it unoccupied in a designated space. Since cars are stationary roughly **95%** of the time, organized parking is a critical component of urban planning and transportation infrastructure.
### 1. Common Parking Layouts
The way vehicles are arranged on a street or in a lot usually falls into three categories:
 * **Parallel Parking:** Vehicles are parked in a line, bumper-to-bumper, parallel to the curb. It requires the least road width but is often the most difficult maneuver for drivers.
 * **Perpendicular Parking:** Cars are parked side-by-side at a 90° angle to the curb or wall. This is the standard layout for most parking lots.
 * **Angle Parking:** Vehicles are parked at an angle (usually 45° or 60°). This is easier to pull into and out of than perpendicular parking and is common in high-traffic retail areas.
### 2. Primary Facilities
 * **On-Street Parking:** Parking located on the sides of public roads. It is convenient but can contribute to traffic congestion.
 * **Off-Street Parking:** Dedicated areas away from the road, such as:
   * **Surface Lots:** Open-air ground-level lots.
   * **Parking Garages:** Multi-level structures that can be above ground, underground, or a mix of both.
   * **Automated Parking Systems (APS):** Mechanical systems that use lifts and pallets to "stack" cars vertically, maximizing space in crowded cities.
### 3. Key Concepts
 * **Public vs. Private:** Public parking is managed by local government (often via meters), while private parking is owned by businesses or residents for specific users.
 * **Parking Restrictions:** Rules governing *where* and *for how long* you can park. These are enforced through signage, permits, or "pay-and-display" tickets.
 * **Smart Parking:** Modern systems that use sensors and mobile apps to show drivers real-time availability, reducing the time spent circling for a spot.
**Why it matters:** Efficient parking management reduces traffic "cruising," lowers carbon emissions, and ensures that emergency vehicles and public transport can move freely through city streets.
